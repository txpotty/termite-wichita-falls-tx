Initial Termite Inspection

The first and most critical step in our termite control process is a detailed inspection. Our trained technicians will conduct a thorough examination of your property, both indoors and outdoors. They are adept at identifying the telltale signs of termite activity, which can often be subtle and difficult for the untrained eye to spot. This inspection is vital for determining the species of termite, the extent of the infestation, and identifying potential entry points and conducive conditions that attract termites.

What We Look For During Inspection

  • Mud Tubes

    Subterranean termites build mud tubes as protective pathways between their underground colonies and their food source (your home's wood). These tubes are a clear indicator of their presence.

  • Damaged Wood

    Termites consume cellulose, which is found in wood. Our technicians will look for signs of damaged wood, such as hollowing, blistering paint, or wood that sounds hollow when tapped.

  • Frass (Termite Droppings)

    Drywood termites produce tiny, pellet-like droppings called frass. Finding small piles of frass can indicate a drywood termite infestation.

  • Discarded Wings

    Termite swarmers, which are reproductive termites, emerge from colonies to mate and establish new colonies. They shed their wings after swarming, and finding discarded wings is a strong sign of a nearby colony.

  • Termite Activity in Crawl Spaces and Basements

    These areas are often damp and provide ideal conditions for termites. Our technicians will pay close attention to these vulnerable areas.

  • Evidence of Termite Activity in Foundation and Slabs

    Termites can enter structures through cracks in the foundation or beneath concrete slabs.

Based on the findings of our inspection, we will develop a customized treatment plan tailored to the specific needs of your property. We believe in transparency and will fully explain our findings and the proposed treatment strategy to you before any work begins.

Signs You May Need Termite Control in Vernon, TX

Termites are often called 'silent destroyers' because they can cause significant damage to your home or business before you even realize they're there. Knowing the signs of a potential termite infestation is crucial for early detection and minimizing the extent of the damage. If you notice any of these indicators in Vernon, TX, it's time to call the experts at Wichita Falls Termite for a thorough inspection and effective treatment.

Common Problems Indicating Termite Activity:

Mud Tubes on Foundation or Walls

These pencil-sized tunnels, often found on exterior or interior walls, basement walls, or crawl spaces, are a clear sign of subterranean termites. They use these tubes to travel between their underground colony and your home's wooden structure, protecting them from predators and dry air. Finding mud tubes, even seemingly inactive ones, warrants immediate investigation.

Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites eat wood from the inside out, leaving a thin veneer of wood or paint on the surface. Tapping on wood surfaces, such as baseboards, door frames, or walls, and hearing a hollow sound can indicate termite damage beneath the surface. This is a strong indicator that termites might be actively feeding on the wood.

Discarded Termite Swarmer Wings

Winged termites, known as swarmers, emerge from mature colonies to reproduce and establish new colonies. They are often mistaken for flying ants. After swarming, they shed their wings. Finding small piles of translucent, discarded wings near windowsills, doorways, or light sources is a definitive sign of a nearby termite colony, and likely an active infestation.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

Significant termite damage to floor joists or structural beams can lead to sagging floors or ceilings. This can be a sign of extensive infestation that requires immediate professional attention to prevent further structural compromise and potential collapse. Addressing this issue promptly is critical for the safety and integrity of your property.

Blistering Paint or Uneven Wall Surfaces

As termites consume wood, they can create tunnels and voids beneath the surface, causing the paint or wallpaper to bubble or blister. Walls may also appear uneven due to the internal wood damage. While blistering paint can have other causes, it's worth investigating if you suspect termite activity.

Small Holes in Wood

While small access holes in wood can be caused by other wood-boring insects, rough, irregular holes can be a sign of drywood termites pushing frass (their droppings) out of their tunnels. Identifying the specific type of hole and the presence of frass is important for accurate diagnosis and treatment.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites produce small, pellet-like droppings called frass, which they push out of their tunnels. These tiny, six-sided pellets are often found in small piles near infested wood. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood the termites are consuming. Finding frass around wooden furniture, window frames, or other wooden structures is a strong indicator of a drywood termite infestation.

Clicking Sounds Within Walls

In some cases, you might be able to hear faint clicking sounds coming from the walls. This sound is made by soldier termites warning the colony when they feel threatened by banging their heads against the wood. While this is a less common sign and can be subtle, if you suspect termite activity and hear such sounds, it warrants a professional inspection.

Tight-Fitting Doors and Windows

Wood damaged by termites can swell and warp, making doors and windows difficult to open or close. This can be a subtle sign that might be overlooked, but coupled with other potential indicators, it can point towards a termite problem. Inspecting the wood around sticking doors and windows for other signs of damage is recommended.

Visible Swarming Activity

The most obvious sign of a termite infestation is seeing actual termite swarmers emerging from your home or business, often in large numbers, particularly during warmer months. Swarmers are attracted to light and may be seen near windows or light fixtures. Seeing swarmers inside your property almost certainly means you have a mature colony nearby that is ready to expand. Do not ignore visible swarming activity; it requires immediate professional attention. Even if you only see a few swarmers, it's crucial to have a professional inspection to determine the source and extent of the infestation.

If you observe any of these signs, it is essential to contact a professional termite control service immediately. Delaying action can lead to more extensive damage and increased repair costs.
